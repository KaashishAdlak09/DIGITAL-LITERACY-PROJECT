UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India. It occurs when criminals trick users into transferring money through digital payment apps such as Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm by exploiting trust, urgency, or lack of awareness.
A common method begins when a fraudster contacts the victim through a phone call, SMS, or social media. The criminal pretends to be a bank representative, customer support agent, or online buyer/seller. Next, the fraudster asks the victim to scan a QR code or approve a “collect request” on their UPI app, claiming it is required to receive money or verify their account. In reality, scanning the QR code or approving the request authorizes a payment from the victim’s bank account to the fraudster. Sometimes criminals also send phishing links that look like legitimate banking websites and trick users into entering their login credentials and OTPs. Once the victim approves the transaction, the money is instantly transferred and is often difficult to recover.
Students, elderly individuals, and people new to digital payments are frequent targets because they may not fully understand how UPI transactions work. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and identity theft if personal details are shared.
According to reports by the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in), thousands of such fraud cases are reported every year in India, highlighting the importance of awareness and digital safety.
